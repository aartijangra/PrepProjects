LOTUS

The Lotus symbolises beauty, purity, and knowledge. This flower grows in muddy waters in ponds, lakes, and shallow water bodies. The lotus has big petals and large round leaves. The flower is considered sacred and is widely used in temples.

IRIS

Iris is a flowering plant with showy flowers. As well as being the scientific name, iris is also widely used as a common name for all Iris species, as well as some belonging to other closely related genera. It is a popular garden flower.

ROSE

A rose is either a woody perennial flowering plant of the genus Rosa, in the family Rosaceae, or the flower it bears.They form a group of plants that can be erect shrubs, climbing, or trailing, with stems that are often armed with sharp prickles.

DAISY

Daisy, is a European species of the family Asteraceae, often considered the archetypal species of the name daisy. To distinguish this species from other plants known as daisies, it is sometimes qualified as common, lawn or English daisy.

TULIPS

Tulips are spring-blooming. Tulipa genus flowers are usually large, showy, and brightly coloured, generally red, orange, pink, yellow, or white. They often have a different coloured blotch at the base of the tepals (petals and sepals, collectively).

SUNFLOWER

The sunflower is a species of large annual forb of the genus Helianthus. It is commonly grown as a crop for its edible oily seeds. Apart from cooking oil production, it is also used as livestock forage, as bird food, and as an ornamental in domestic gardens.

JASMINE

Jasmine can be either deciduous or evergreen, and can be erect, spreading, or climbing shrubs and vines. Their leaves are borne in opposing or alternating arrangement and can be of simple or pinnate formation.

LILY

Lilies are tall perennials, 2 to 6 feet tall. They form naked or tunicless scaly underground bulbs, which are their reproductive organs. In some North American species, rhizomes develop from the base of a bulb containing many small bulbs.
